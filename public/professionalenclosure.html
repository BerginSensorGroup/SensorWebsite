Overview

  • Item Name : Low Cost Air Quality Sensors
  • Item Version : v 1.0

This documentation should outline all of the equipment and steps needed to prepare the Bergin lab low-cost air quality sensors. We have put these packages together in two different ways:

  • Faster but potentially more expensive: Have a company build the printed circuit boards and provide them to us with a lot of the parts already soldered on (you can either provide a bill of materials, outlining all of the additional parts that need to be purchased and where they can be purchased from, or you can send some or all of these parts directly to the PCB manufacturer to solder onto the boards)
  • Cheaper but slower: Order the PCBs, purchase all of the parts separately and solder the parts onto the boards ourselves in the lab

If you are doing this in India (or anywhere outside of the US) you would need to work with a PCB printing company to make sure they can print our design. We have worked with the same company a few times now so have worked out the problems, but it was a slow process to start. I have provided the files that we sent to the PCB company along with this documentation.
